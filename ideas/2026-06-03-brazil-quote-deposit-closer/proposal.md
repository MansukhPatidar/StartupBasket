# FechaJá — PIX-deposit closer for Brazil home-service pros

## 1. One-liner

WhatsApp agent that chases your sent orçamentos and locks the job with a PIX sinal — you just show up.

## 2. Trend signal — why now?

Three things lined up in the last 12 months, and they point at the same Brazilian small-trade operator bleeding money on quotes that go silent.

**The pain is named and ranked in Portuguese.** Brazilian SMB-marketing sites run whole articles titled "Cliente pediu orçamento e sumiu?" ("Client asked for a quote and vanished?"). SocialHub: *"Ver um lead pedir um orçamento e desaparecer é um dos maiores pesadelos de qualquer gestor comercial."* And *"Cada orçamento esquecido é um investimento em tempo e recursos jogado fora."* When an SEO-driven content marketer writes that headline, it's because the search volume is there.

**PIX Automático became mandatory for participating banks in October 2025** (Agência Brasil), on top of the June-2025 launch. Brazil now has a free/near-free, account-to-account instant rail that can collect a deposit (*sinal*) the moment a client says yes — 14× cheaper than card, no acquirer, works for any MEI with a CNPJ-linked PIX key. The "charge a deposit at booking" mechanic that drops aesthetic-clinic no-shows ~70% is now available to a painter or electrician for the cost of a PIX.

**US voice/AI for the trades is a funded land-grab** — voice-AI funding surged ~8× to $2.1B in 2025; Avoca alone is on track to book ~$1B in jobs for trade customers in 2026. The whole category (Avoca, Numa, QuoteIQ, BuildFolio, WarmRing) is **English-first, card/ServiceTitan-centric, and US-only.** None of it speaks Portuguese, lives in WhatsApp, or collects a PIX sinal. That's the arbitrage.

## 3. The opportunity

A Brazilian home-service pro — pintor, eletricista, marceneiro, empreiteiro de reforma — runs his whole sales process inside WhatsApp. A lead messages, he visits, he sends an orçamento as a photo or PDF, and then… nothing. He's on a ladder; he doesn't have the time, the system, or honestly the stomach to chase. Industry follow-up math is brutal and universal: most sales need 5–12 touches, and roughly half of senders never follow up even once. In Brazil the lead itself was often *bought* — GetNinjas pros pay R$12–30 per unlocked contact and then watch them ghost, with no refund.

The gap: US tools solve this but don't fit. Brazilian software (seuFisio, Vedius, ZenFisio, Clínica Ágil) solves *recurring* billing for gyms and clinics — monthly mensalidades — not **one-off project-quote recovery** for trades. WhatsApp CRMs (SocialHub) sell broad cadence tooling but nothing trade-shaped that closes the loop with a deposit. So the specific job — "chase my sent orçamento and lock it with a sinal" — is owned by nobody. A focused, Portuguese, WhatsApp-native, PIX-first agent can own it before the US players bother to localize (they're busy eating the US).

The 10× isn't a prettier dashboard. It's: the pro does *zero* follow-up and still books more jobs, because the agent runs the cadence and — the kill feature — converts a "talvez" into a paid sinal that self-selects out the ghosters.

## 4. Target market

- **Primary customer:** Solo or micro home-service operators in Brazil — pintores, eletricistas, marceneiros, encanadores, empreiteiros de reforma. MEI or small (1–5 people), R$15K–80K/month revenue, quoting 20–60 jobs/month, all over WhatsApp.
- **Why they buy (their words):** *"Cliente pediu orçamento e sumiu."* They paid for the lead (often literally, on GetNinjas), they did the site visit, they sent the number — and it died in silence. Reclame Aqui is full of GetNinjas pros furious that *"os profissionais pagam, mas o cliente não responde"* with no refund. They're not buying CRM; they're buying "stop letting jobs I already worked for rot."
- **Rough TAM reasoning:** Brazil has millions of MEIs; home-services/reform is one of the largest informal trade categories, and GetNinjas/Cronoshare/Triider list hundreds of thousands of service pros. Even 50–100K reachable, WhatsApp-fluent, quote-sending pros at R$79/mo is a R$50–100M ARR ceiling — far past the $5M target, with room to be picky.
- **Why now for them:** PIX Automático/PIX deposits just made "cobre o sinal" frictionless and culturally normal; clients already pay everything by PIX. And lead costs keep rising, so the cost of a ghosted quote is now visceral and quantified.

## 5. Product sketch (MVP)

- Connect WhatsApp; forward or snap a photo of any orçamento you send — FechaJá registers it as an open quote.
- Auto-runs a Portuguese follow-up cadence (Day 0 confirm → Day 1–2 reforço → Day 3–4 nudge → Day 5–7 "ainda tem interesse?") that sounds like a polite, sharp salesperson, never guilt-trips ("nunca faça o cliente sentir culpa").
- One-tap **PIX sinal**: agent offers the client a deposit link/QR; on payment, the job auto-moves to "fechado" and the cadence stops.
- Objection handling in PT: answers "tá caro", "vou pensar", "me manda depois" with pro-set scripts and re-quote floors you define.
- Simple pipeline board: Aberto / Aquecendo / Sinal pago / Perdido — and *why* it was lost.
- Daily WhatsApp digest to the pro: "3 quentes hoje, 1 sinal pago (R$ 400), 2 esfriando."
- Re-engagement of cold/old quotes: "você tem 14 orçamentos parados há +30 dias — quer que eu tente de novo?"

## 6. AI angle — what's load-bearing

Remove the AI and this is a dumb drip sequence — which is exactly what the all-in-one suites already ship and pros ignore. The AI is load-bearing in three places: (1) **reading the orçamento** — pulling the job, the value, and the client name from a photo/PDF/voice note so the pro does zero data entry; (2) **conversational follow-up in real Brazilian Portuguese** — handling "tá caro / vou ver com meu marido / me chama semana que vem" like a human closer, in-thread, without sounding like a bot; (3) **deciding the next move** — when to nudge, when to offer the sinal, when to back off, when to mark dead. The whole pitch is "you do nothing and it still closes," and only an agent that converses and decides delivers that. A form with reminders does not.

## 7. Localization angle (if any)

This *is* the localization play — it's the entire moat. Portuguese-native conversation (not translated US scripts). WhatsApp as the OS (not SMS/email, which Brazilian trades barely use for sales). **PIX sinal** as the close mechanic — there is no US analog; PIX Automático/instant PIX is the unlock the English-first incumbents structurally can't copy without re-architecting around a rail they don't have. Pricing in reais (R$79/mo lands where $49/mo would feel insane to an MEI). Distribution through the same channels these pros already live in — GetNinjas/Triider seller communities, WhatsApp groups, vernacular trade creators on YouTube/Instagram.

## 8. Business model — path to $1M–$5M ARR

- **Pricing:** R$79/mo solo (Essencial); R$149/mo Pro (multi-user, custom scripts, re-engagement of old quotes, priority). Optional micro-take on collected sinais (e.g. 1%) later — but launch on flat subscription to keep it dead simple.
- **ACV:** ~R$950–1,800/year (≈$170–320). Anchor: pros already burn R$12–30 *per lead* on GetNinjas and lose most; R$79/mo to actually close the ones they bought is a no-brainer ROI story.
- **Math to R$5M ARR (~$900K, the "$1M" rung):** ~5,300 pros × R$79/mo × 12. At a population of 50K+ reachable quote-senders, that's ~10% penetration — achievable for a focused niche tool.
- **Math to $5M ARR:** ~R$27M ARR ≈ ~28K pros on a blended ~R$80/mo, or fewer pros if the Pro tier and sinal take-rate lift ACV. Requires expanding beyond reform into adjacent quote-heavy trades (fotógrafos, festas/eventos, serviços automotivos) — same engine, new verticals.
- **Expansion path:** solo → Pro (team) → sinal take-rate → adjacent verticals → "fechado" analytics and benchmarking the pro will pay to keep.

## 9. Go-to-market wedge — first 100 customers

- **Mine the angry GetNinjas/Triider Reclame Aqui threads.** There are public, dated complaints from pros (eletricistas, reformas) furious that paid leads ghost with no refund. DM/comment a 40-second PT Loom: "você pagou pelo lead e o cliente sumiu — eu cobro o sinal e fecho pra você." Warm, specific, painful — expect a real reply rate.
- **WhatsApp/Telegram and Facebook groups for MEIs and trades** (pintores, marceneiros, reforma). Post a free "calculadora de orçamentos perdidos": enter quotes/month → see R$ left on the table. Lead magnet → trial.
- **Vernacular trade creators on YouTube/Instagram** (the "como precificar reforma / como fechar orçamento" niche has dedicated PT channels). Sponsor 3–5 mid-tier creators whose audience *is* the customer; they demo the sinal close on camera.
- **Direct sinal demo:** onboarding sends the pro's *own* next real quote through the cadence as a free trial — first booked sinal is the activation moment and the testimonial.

## 10. Build complexity — justification

Medium. The web stack, follow-up engine, and pipeline board are off-the-shelf; PT conversation runs on commodity LLMs that are already strong in Brazilian Portuguese. The two real lifts are (a) **WhatsApp Business API** integration done well (templates, session windows, approval) and (b) **PIX collection** plumbing — but PIX provider APIs (and PIX Automático rails) are mature and well-documented in Brazil, and the deposit flow is a link/QR, not a custom payment processor. A small team ships a credible v1 in ~3–4 months; no novel ML, no hardware, no data moat to acquire.

## 13. Qualitative modifiers

### Founder-fit tags

`technical-heavy` (WhatsApp + PIX + agent plumbing) · `sales-heavy` (PT trade GTM, creator deals, community selling). Ideally a Brazil-based or Brazil-fluent builder.

### Key assumptions to validate (3–5)

1. **Assumption:** Pros will let an AI run their client follow-up in *their* WhatsApp voice without fear of looking spammy. **How to test:** 20 in-person/Zoom interviews with reform pros; show the cadence transcripts, watch for "isso parece eu" vs "isso parece robô."
2. **Assumption:** Offering a PIX sinal materially lifts close rate (not just filters out tire-kickers to zero). **How to test:** Run 30 real quotes through manual cadence-with-sinal vs without; compare booked R$.
3. **Assumption:** R$79/mo clears for solo MEIs given they already pay per lead. **How to test:** Price-test in onboarding; measure trial→paid at R$49 vs R$79 vs R$149.
4. **Assumption:** Reclame Aqui / MEI-group outreach converts cheaply enough. **How to test:** 200 targeted DMs/comments; measure reply → demo → trial.

### Risk flags

1. **Platform dependency:** Heavy reliance on WhatsApp Business API policy (template approval, messaging limits, bans for perceived spam). A policy shift could throttle the cadence engine.
2. **Incumbent localization:** A funded US player (or a Brazilian payment company like Cora/PagBrasil/Efí) could bolt quote-recovery onto an existing PIX product and outspend a bootstrapper.
3. **Market timing / informality:** Some MEIs underreport income and may resist a paper trail of paid sinais; informal-cash habits could cap willingness to formalize deposits.
4. **Adjacent overlap:** Brazilian all-in-one suites could extend recurring-billing tooling into one-off quote recovery — but their DNA is mensalidades, so reaction is likely slow.
